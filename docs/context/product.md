# Product

## Purpose

This document defines the product vision for Orakl. It exists to provide context for anyone contributing to the platform, whether they are a developer, designer or AI assistant.

Implementation details such as frameworks, APIs and databases are intentionally excluded from this document. Its purpose is to explain **what Orakl is**, **who it is for**, and **the principles that guide its evolution**.

---

# Vision

Orakl is a social competitive gaming platform designed to make organising and participating in friendly competitions effortless.

It enables groups of colleagues, friends and communities to compete against one another through a variety of games that combine knowledge, strategy, prediction and luck.

The platform removes the administrative overhead traditionally associated with organising competitions, allowing organisers and participants to focus on playing rather than managing spreadsheets, calculating scores or maintaining standings.

---

# Mission

Orakl exists to bring people together through competition.

Its goal is to strengthen friendly rivalries by providing simple, engaging and automated experiences that encourage participation before, during and after an event.

Whether the competition lasts a single afternoon or spans an entire tournament, Orakl should make organising and participating feel effortless.

---

# Target Audience

Orakl is designed primarily for:

* Workplace teams and colleagues
* Friend groups
* Social communities

The initial focus is workplace competitions, where organisers often rely on spreadsheets and manual administration.

As the platform evolves, it is intended to become a recognised destination for social competitions across friend groups and wider communities.

---

# The Problem Orakl Solves

Organising competitions is often more work than participating in them.

Existing solutions frequently require organisers to:

* Build and maintain spreadsheets.
* Calculate scores manually.
* Track standings.
* Update brackets and fixtures.
* Resolve mistakes by hand.

This administrative burden discourages people from creating competitions.

Orakl automates these responsibilities wherever possible, allowing organisers to focus on running enjoyable competitions instead of managing data.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Orakl aims to become the go-to platform for organising social competitions.

Initially this means supporting workplace events such as sports tournaments and major sporting competitions.

Over time the platform should naturally extend into friend groups, clubs and communities, becoming a recognised destination for competitive social gaming regardless of the game being played.

---

# Product Values

Every feature added to Orakl should reinforce the following principles.

## Competition First

Competition is at the heart of every experience. Features should encourage participation, engagement and friendly rivalry.

## Easy to Organise

Creating and managing competitions should require as little administrative effort as possible.

Automation should replace manual processes whenever it can do so reliably.

## Simple to Participate

Players should be able to join competitions quickly, understand the rules immediately and spend their time playing rather than learning the platform.

## Built for Groups

Every decision should consider the experience of organisers and participants as a group rather than as individual users.

## Extensible by Design

Orakl is a platform rather than a single game.

New competitive experiences should be able to integrate into the platform without changing its core purpose or identity.

---

# Definition of Orakl

Orakl is a platform for organising and playing competitive social games.

It provides the shared systems required to create, manage and participate in competitions while allowing individual games to define their own rules, scoring and gameplay.

The platform exists to make competition fun, accessible and effortless to organise.
